Etienne Hanelt

DPhil candidate

University of Oxford

I’m a political scientist working on autocratization in Hungary and Poland and the European Union’s actions to protect democracy, rule of law, and judicial independence in Member States. I’m affiliated with the University of Oxford and Wolfson College as a DPhil candidate and with the Judicial Studies Institute (JUSTIN) at Masaryk University as a researcher. As part of my mixed-method work, I trace political processes, using both written sources and semi-structured elite and expert interviews and complement this with insights from computational methods, which I use to collect and analyse digital texts. I am also interested in U.S. and South Korean politics.

At JUSTIN, I am a member of an international and interdisciplinary team on the ERC-funded INFINITY research project – ‘Informal Judicial Institutions: Invisible Determinants of Democratic Decay’.

At the University of Oxford, I was one of the founders and convenors of a graduate discussion group, OxonCourts, that served as an interdisciplinary forum for researchers working on judicial institutions in Oxford. We organized regular talks with invited speakers and other academic events and raised funds from both the Department of Politics and International Relations as well as the Faculty of Law. In 2018-19, I was the Journal Secretary for the Journal of the Oxford Centre for Socio-Legal Studies (JOxCSLS, 2018–19). Before that, I volunteered as a legal researcher on comparative human rights for Oxford Pro Bono Publico.

I was for three years actively participating in the governance of Wolfson College, representing non-tenured researchers and graduate students. I was elected as the Chair of the General Meeting – the Common Room President – in 2019 and participated in this capacity in many committees: Academic Committee, Academic Proposals and Funding Sub-Committee, Communications Sub-Committee, Domestic and Premises Committee, Equality and Welfare Committee, Social and Cultural Committee as well as the inter-collegiate Graduate Presidents Committee. I was also twice elected to the Governing Body (2019–21) and served for two years in its executive body, the General Purposes Committee (2018–20). This involved me in virtually all aspects of College governance and strategy.

I hold degrees in Political Science from Friedrich Schiller University Jena (MA, BA) and have studied as a graduate exchange student in Michigan and South Korea. My master’s thesis was awarded the Jena Political Science Department’s best thesis award in 2016. Before joining Oxford, I taught classes in political science at Jena.
